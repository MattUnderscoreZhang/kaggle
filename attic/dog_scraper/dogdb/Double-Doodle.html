Overview
The Double Doodle is a mix of Goldendoodle and Labradoodle or various mixtures of Golden Retriever, Labrador retriever and Poodle. This mix has also been called the North American Retriever and Golden Labradoodle. This breed is intelligent, well-rounded, and has been cited as an affable family pet. It is a medium size dog and has weight 30 to 70 pounds. Their life expectancy is 12 to 15 years. Typically Double doodles are trainable and obedient dogs with a great intelligence. Exercise is very necessary for the fitness and the health of a dog. A fair amount to high level of exercise may required by this hybrid dog.
Body type
The Double Doodle is considered a large sized dog breed. An adult Double Doodle will weigh between 30-70 pounds and stand 20-24 inches tall from ground to shoulder.
Coat
The coat of double doodle may be any type rough, silky, wiry or it may tend to be wavy, curly or straight.
Color
It may have various different coat colors of parent dogs as cream, white, apricot, red, gold, black, chocolate, silver or parti-colors
Temperament
This cool-headed, family-oriented, sociable dog loves being around people it loves – its owner, family, children and other pets, with whom they would mix well, but would bark at strangers to alert its family. They are adjustable with all climates, however they do not do well in cold weathers. Basically, their personality might vary from individual to individual.
Exercise
Exercise is very necessary for the fitness and the health of a dog. A fair amount to high level of exercise may required by this hybrid dog.
Health
As a hybrid dog breed, the Double Doodle will have more genetic diversity than its purebred ancestors which helps with the breed's health and hardiness. However, the breed does suffer from health issues common in both the Labradoodle and the Goldendoodle, including hip dysplasia, cataracts, and bloat. The life expectancy for this breed is 12-15 years.
