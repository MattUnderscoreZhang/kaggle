Overview
Karelian Bear Dog is a medium-sized, solidly built, Spitz-type dog, with a triangular shaped head, prick ears, and a hard, straight coat, black or black and white medium length. The length of the body from the point of buttocks to the tip of the sternum, is slightly longer than the height measured at the withers. Coat slightly longer on the thighs can make the length of the body seem larger. The tail is carried in a loose curl over the back, although a natural or bob tail stub is acceptable. Honorable scars or other evidence of injury arising from work in the field, should not be penalized.
Body type
Karelian Bear Dog is slightly longer, measured from prosternum to point of buttocks, than height, measured from the withers to the ground. The topline slopes slightly downward from well-developed withers and then becomes level. The back is well muscled, with a short back, muscular, strong and broad and slightly sloping croup. Ribs moderately sprung from the spine, forming a strong back. The chest is moderately broad chest and extends to the elbow. The forechest should extend in a shallow oval shape in front of the forelegs but the sternum should not be excessively pointed. There is a slight but perceptible raised.
Coat
Karelian Bear Dog has a double coat of medium length, with the hair on the back of the neck and buttocks slightly longer, and the hair of the head, feet and ears a little shorter. The outer layer is thick, straight, and is a bit away from the body. The undercoat is dense, soft and dense.
Color
Black, usually sharp-edged white spots or marks on the head, neck, chest, belly and legs. Brown-black, matte black and glossy black are all acceptable.
Temperament
Karelian Bear Dog has a quiet temperament, but stable, and is very dedicated to his teacher. Karelians have strong hunting instincts. They tend to be aggressive with other dogs, especially in their own territory, so they do not work well in a package. They are brave and confident, and attack bear and other big game animals without hesitation. His senses, especially smell, are very interested. They are never aggressive towards people.
Gait
Karelian Bear Dog moves with steps of moderate length, with a march that conveys vitality and agility rather than power and strength. It is a quick and agile trot that allows the dog to switch effortlessly to gallop, which is more natural gait of the breed. Correct movement is essential in this breed.
