Overview
Mudi is a baby sized dog with Spitz-like qualities. It has a wedge shaped head, prick ears, moderate bone and a compact body with a sloping topline. The face and the front of the legs are covered with short hair, smooth, while the rest of the coat is somewhat long and very wavy to curly. Mudi comes in a variety of different colors.
Body type
Mudi typically weigh 18-29 pounds (8.2 to 13 kg) and stand 15 to 19 inches (38-48 cm) tall at the withers. The coat is medium wavy or curly, with short hair on the face and legs. [1] The accepted colors are black (fekete), brown (barna), white (Fehér), yellow (Falco), gray (Hamvas) graybrown (hamvasbarna), black merle (Net), brown merle (barna figure), gray merle (Hamvas figure) and graybrown merle (hamvasbarna figure). [2] Mudi have short tails that may be born long and docked short or born naturally short.
Coat
The hair on the face and front of legs is short, straight and smooth. In the rest of the body, the coat is thick and shiny, very wavy or slightly curled, and about 1.5 to 3 inches in length. No pronounced feathering on the back of the front legs and upper thighs.
Color
Solid and self-merle colors: Black, white, fawn (ranging from pale yellow to fox red), brown, gray, gray-brown, blue merle and red merle.
Temperament
The Mudi is lively, gentle, intelligent and eager. Very brave, the Mudi is extremely popular among the shepherds and is often used in large and difficult livestock. He makes an exceptional guard, guard and alarm dog, and is an excellent agility dog ​​and every neighbor.
Gait
At a trot, the Mudi takes short, quick lively steps. The Mudi should appear to be smooth, firm and agile in action and any clumsiness or rigidity in motion should be faulted.
