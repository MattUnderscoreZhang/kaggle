Overview
The Presa Canario is a dog of moderate size, powerfully built with a harsh-textured, flat coat. The head is massive and cuboid. The ears are located just above the eye line can be cut and naturally pendant or pink. The body of the Presa Canario is slightly longer than the height at the withers, with a wide and deep chest, but females may be slightly longer and possess somewhat less rib spring than males. The skin is thick and elastic. The distance from the withers to the elbow is approximately equal to the distance from the elbow to the ground. The tail is very thick at the base and tapers to a point. The tail is moderately high and reaches to the hock. The overall impression is that of a watchdog imposing solid.
Body type
A correctly proportioned Presa is 20% longer (measured from prosternum to point of buttocks) than tall (measured from the withers to the ground), and length of the front leg (measured from point of elbow to the ground) should be equal to about half the height of the dog. Females may be slightly longer in the body. The cross is slightly elevated. The back and loin are strong but without prominent musculature. The bottom line rises slightly toward the croup, which is only slightly higher than the withers. The croup is of medium length, broad and slightly sloping. Women normally have slightly wider rumps. The loin is moderately short, muscular and deep, with moderate tuck-up. The ribs are well sprung. The brisket extends to the elbow. The chest is broad and deep, with well developed pectoral muscles. Ideally, the circumference of the deeper part of the chest should equal almost 1 ½ times the height of the dog at the withers.
Coat
La Presa has a short flat layer, which is roughly only in texture. It is very short and fine in the ears and a little longer on the cross and the back of the rear hindquarters.

Temperament
The essential characteristics of the Presa are those that let you manage and keep livestock, and to protect your home and family. These tasks require a powerful agility, dog, brave, with a large head and powerful jaws. The dam is very affectionate, docile and well behaved with its owner and his family, but suspicious of strangers and aggressive with other dogs.
Gait
When trotting, the stride is long, effortless and well coordinated and the tail is carried above the backline.
