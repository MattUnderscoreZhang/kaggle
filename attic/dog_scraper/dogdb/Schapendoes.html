Overview
Schapendoes is a medium-sized, lightly built, long haired dog, which is equipped with a high strength, mobility and speed. Their movement is elastic and it is a remarkable bridge.
Body type
The Schapendoes is a medium sized dog, with long, thick hair on the body, legs, tail and face. Small ears hanging, covered with long hair. The face has a mustache and beard. The coat is of any color.

The height is up to 50 cm (19.7 inches) at the withers and 12 to 20 kg (26-44 pounds), 25 kg (55 lbs) for males, by weight.
Coat
Denso, double and slightly wavy. Approximately 3 inches long in the region of the hindquarters. Definitely curly or frizzy is unacceptable. The hairs are thin and dry, never silky. The fur is sometimes off in clumps. There is a final bow, mustache and beard.

Temperament
The breed standard describes the Schapendoes as friendly, high-spirited, and affectionate. There is an aggressive guard dog or protection, and if well socialized when they are young, it is likely to make a good family dog ​​and a good dog for active sports. Lively and intelligent dogs should receive regular training and outputs. Temperament of individual dogs may vary.
Gait
The natural gait of the Schapendoes is a gallop, not a trot. He must be light-footed and springy, and not waste energy.
