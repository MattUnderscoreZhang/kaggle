Overview
The Spanish Water Dog is a well balanced dog of medium size and bone, slightly longer than tall, with a distinctive wool coat curls and forms cords;, triangular drop ears high set, and a tail that is naturally bobtail or coupled between the second and fourth vertebrae. In both sexes, masculinity or femininity is well defined.
Body type
The Spanish Water Dog is a medium-sized dog. The approximate measurements are:

Males
Height (at the withers): 44 to 50 cm (17 to 20 in)
Weight: 18 to 22 kg (40 to 49 lb)

Females
Height (at the withers): 40 to 45 cm (16 to 18 in)
Weight: 14 to 18 kg (31 to 40 lb)
Coat
It has a distinctive curly hair is woolly in texture and may form cords when long. The hair should not be cut or prepared for aesthetic purposes. Instead, it should be completely natural, as if you are not prepared at all. It should never be cut, but cut at least once a year. SWD puppies always are born with curly hair.
Color
Black & White, White, Brown, Black, Beige, Brown & White
Temperament
The Spanish Water Dog is a working dog smart and versatile, with a strong herding, hunting and guardian instincts. He is an exceptional companion, attentive and animated, showing strength and stamina combined with unusual agility. It is versatile and easily trained, performing his assigned tasks with competence and dignity. It is reserved with strangers, but should not show shyness. Although an employee authorized, cruelty to people or animals is intolerable.
Gait
Smooth, easy and free; exhibiting agility of movement with a well-balanced, calm earth covered. Front and hind legs move straight and parallel to the centerline of the body, which increases the speed, the feet, both front and rear, converge toward the center line of gravity of the dog, while the topline remains firm and level.
