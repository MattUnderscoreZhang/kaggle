Overview
The Sussex spaniel is one of AKC first recognized 10 species in the 1884 issue of variety Handbook, but as a unique species, it appears earlier. As its name implies, plug, which originated in England, from the beginning of eighteenth Century, a dog in field application. In late 1800, the Sussex Spaniel as an excellent hunting companion dog, around the county of Sussex has become quite famous. Short legs, massive, long body, smell the smell used to cry, very suitable for the dense bushes, with sharp bark in the rifle range. Powerful, flexible, love is the basic requirement of varieties. Although, in number could not be popularized, but from a sporting dog nineteenth Century until today, the characteristics and features of almost no change.

The Sussex spaniel is long and low, rectangular and massive structure, combined with a stretch of gait and nice tail action. It has a somber and serious expression. The rich golden liver color is unique to the breed.
