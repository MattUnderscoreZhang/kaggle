Overview
American White Shepherd Club of Canada (WSCC) has been dedicated to the promotion and preservation of the White Shepherd since 1971. Originally formed as a Chapter of the White German Shepherd Dog Club of America, the club was renamed White Shepherd Club of Canada in 1973. His show was held the first formation of this year with 8 dogs entered and 25 people attended.
Body type
American White Shepherd, as recognized by UKC, is a medium-sized, well-balanced, muscular dog, slightly longer than tall, with a medium length, pure white coat, erect ears and a low-set natural tail that normally reaches to the hock and is carried in a slight curve like a saber. The White Shepherd is solid without volume and should be shown in poor condition, physical drive. The white contour Shepherd include gentle curves instead of angles. When trotting, the White Shepherd moves with a long stride and efficient which is driven by a powerful forward thrust from the hindquarters. The back leg forward, changes under the foreleg and touches the ground at the place where the forefoot left an imprint. Gender differences are evident.
Coat
American White Shepherd has a weather-resistant double coat. The outer coat is dense, straight, harsh, and close lying. The undercoat is short, thick, and fine in texture. At the neck, the coat may be slightly longer and heavier, particularly in males. Ideal coat color is a pure white.
