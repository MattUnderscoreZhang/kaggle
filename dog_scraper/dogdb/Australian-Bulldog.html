Overview
The Australian Bulldog, originally known as the Aussie Bulldog, is a dog breed developed from Australia. Selective breeding began in the 1990s by breeders who want to create a dog with the look of a Bulldog, but without the problems inherent health reproduced. There were two independent breeding programs, one by Noel and Tina Green (the JAG line) and a separate program by Pip Nobes (the Nobes Australian Bulldog / Wingara lines). Each has its own separate records. The only incorporated and constituted body for the breed is the Australian Bulldog Society or ABS, founded by Nobes PIP. The ABS will only be recorded on Australian Bulldog Australian Bulldog litters are being carried out its first census of 5 years working for ABS Australian Bulldogs being recognized by the ANKC as a breed. The United Aussie Bulldog UABA Association (formed in 2003) is directed by Noel and Tina Green, this registry is still actively registering British Bulldog over Aussie Bulldog litters.
Body type
The Australian Bulldog is a breed of stocky medium sized. Men standing of 40-45 cm and a weight of 28-35 kg, females 40-48 cm stand at 25-35 kg weight. The skull is large but in proportion to their body. The muzzle is broad and wide, short, but not to the extent that it interferes with breathing. The muzzle should be one to two wrinkles and bite can be a little overwhelmed or level. The eyes are set low and far apart, and can be of any color. The ears should be small and medium enterprises, and can be rosebud or buttons. Long neck thick and deep, slightly longer than the British bulldog for his breathing is unimpaired. The back is short and straight, front legs wide apart, but still below the body and straight. The hindquarters are muscular and back yards and compact. The tail is set high, thick at the base. A short tail or long crank bolt are acceptable.
Coat
The coat is short and smooth. The Australian Bulldog comes in a variety of colors, including shades of white, mahogany, red, orange, apricot, fawn and brindle five shades of silver, including brindle, mahogany brindle, black brindle, brindle fawn, brindle and red.

Gait
Australian Bulldog has the ability to run and kill a full grown sheep. It is not recommended to be kept around livestock. Australian Bulldogs are considered as an indoor dog and work best in temperate climates, nothing too hot or cold.
