Overview
The Bolognese ( Bichon Bolognese ) is a small breed of dog of the Bichon type, origin: Italy. The name refers to the central Italian city of Bologna. Bolognese are pure white without any markings or shadings. The Bolognese is hypoallergenic. It is part of the Toy dog group and is considered a companion dog. They love attention, and make good house pets. He is not especially active, but he will tailor his activity level to yours. His whole goal in life is to spend time with you and make you happy. The average life span of the Bolognese is 14 years but they have been known to live up to 18 years.
Body type
The Bolognese's height varies 10.5 to 12 inches for a male and 10-11 inches for a female.The weight varies between 6 to 14 lbs. It has large, dark eyes and nose, square muzzle, white teeth, pincer or scissors bite, and long, highly set ears. Bolognese have a deep chest and tail curled over the back. The Bolognese is a small, squarish dog which is about as tall as it is wide.
Coat
The hair is long and rather fluffy on the entire dog; somewhat shorter only on the muzzle. It lies in flocks, not fringe. The coat is shown natural length, with no trimming except for neatening around the bottom of the feet and for hygienic purposes. The Bolognese is hypoallergenic.
Color
Pure white, with no patching or shading of any other color.
Temperament
Beagliers are good-natured dogs that feature a calm disposition. This wonderful breed for kids is known to be playful, energetic, and affectionate around them. It is also shy towards other pets.They make good watchdogs and they will bark or notify their family as soon as they hear or sense something that doesn’t seem right.
Exercise / Training
While Bolognese are perfectly happy to lounge around the house, they should also have a daily walk. A good walk would be around 20-25 minutes once or twice a week, but 10 minute walks can suffice for daily walks.

The Bolognese is eager to please and usually easy to train. It does well with obedience training, especially when young.
