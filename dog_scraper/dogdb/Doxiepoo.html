Overview
The Doxiepoo (Doodle, Doxie Poo) is a medium cross breed. It is the cross of the Dachshund and Poodle. They meet in verity of colors but most common are black and tan, black, red, brown and white. They may have the best traits of their parents. People oriented Doxiepoos may have low and very close to the ground body. They are loveable and nice with children but supervision is necessary. Loyal to the family doxiepoos are affectionate and easy to train. Friendly Doxiepoos are intelligent and loving it makes good family pet. Their height is variable and weight is normally from 10 pounds to 30 pounds and life expectancy is 12 to 15 years.
Body type
Medium mixed breed dog, The height of the Doxie Poo variable and weight normally is 10 to 30 pounds.
Coat
They can have short or long coat that can be rough or fluffy and curly or wavy in nature.
Color
The most common colors for Doxiepoos are black, black and tan, brown, gray, and white.
Temperament
The Doxiepoo is likely to be an active and mischievous dog with a sense of humor.  but affectionate and loyal to the family. They are good with small children and pets but supervision is necessary. Friendly, loyal and affectionate Doxie poos are loveable also. They make ag great family dog. They may love to dig and to bark and can composed a good watch dog because they are also alert and attentive. Good with all ages, alert and watchful Doxie Poo may alert the family regarding approaching visitors and unusual sounds. These dogs love to be around their family and make a good companion of heir people.
 Training
These dog are require a moderate amount of exercises to be healthy and fit. A daily walk, run or jog is enough for these hybrid dogs.

They are easy to train dogs with their inherited intelligence but sometimes, they can show stubborn behavior of a Dachshund. This should be dealt with firm and consistent training sessions which should be followed with food and love reward reinforcements.
Health
The Doxiepoo may suffer from skin and nasal allergies. The Doxiepoo's ears need to be kept clean and dry, so clean the ears regularly with a gentle ar cleaning solution recommended by your veterinarian. The rest is basic care. Trim the nails as needed, usually every week or two.
