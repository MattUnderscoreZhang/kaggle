Overview
The Icelandic Sheepdog is a little under sized Spitz-type dog with a triangular shaped head, prick ears and a double layer thickness. Seen from the side, the dog is rectangular. The body length from the shoulder point to the tip of the buttocks is far greater than the height at withers. The chest is equal to the length of the foreleg. The tail is carried in a loose curl over the back. There are two types of layer, long and short, and both are thick and extremely weatherproof. There is a marked difference in appearance between the sexes. The Icelandic Sheepdog should be evaluated as grazing and drover dog, and exaggerations or faults should be penalized in proportion to how much interfere with the dog's ability to work.
Body type
The Icelandic Sheepdog is slightly longer than tall. The defensive line is level. The back is well muscled and joins with a broad and muscular back. The croup is rather short and wide, and slightly sloping. Ribs moderately outwardly arched spine. The chest is broad and deep. The forechest should extend in a shallow oval shape in front of the forelegs but the sternum should not be excessively pointed. There is a slight raised.
Coat
The Icelandic Sheepdog has a double coat. The outer layer is quite thick, straight, and may remain flat or stand slightly away from the body. The undercoat is dense, soft and dense. The hair on the face, topskull, ears and front of legs is shorter than the layer on the body, while the layer in the neck, chest, back and thighs of the front legs is slightly longer . The tail is bushy.

Temperament
Icelandic sheepdogs are tough and energetic. Hardy and agile, they are very useful for herding and driving livestock or finding lost sheep. However, dogs are not known for hunting. Icelandic Sheepdogs are very alert and will always give visitors an enthusiastic welcome without being aggressive. Friendly and cheerful, the Icelandic sheepdog is inquisitive, playful and unafraid. They usually get along well with children and other pets.
Gait
Correct movement is essential in this breed. The Icelandic Sheepdog moves in steps of moderate length at a fast trot, agile, essential for driving on rough terrain actions of Iceland. The fore and hind legs are carried straight, elbows and knee joints either inward or outward. While the dog is in motion, the topline remains firm and appears level.
