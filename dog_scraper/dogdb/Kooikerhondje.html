Overview
The Kooikerhondje is a medium sized dog, slightly longer than tall, resembling a small setter or spaniel. The ears are set high and low with a distinctive black stripe on the tips. The coat is of medium length and slightly wavy, white with bright orange to deep red spots. The tail feathers are mostly white and well. Exaggerations or faults should be penalized in proportion to the degree of deviation from the standard.
Body type
A properly Kooikerhondje provided is only slightly longer (measured from prosternum to point of buttocks) than tall (measured from the withers to the ground), and the length of the front leg (measured from point of elbow to the ground) should be equal to about half the height of the dog. If the dog is standing or moving, the backline is strong and level from withers to slightly sloping croup. The loin is moderately short, muscular and deep, with moderately raised. The ribs extend well back and are well sprung out of the spine, forming a broad back, strong, then curving downwardly and inwardly to form a deep body. The skirt extends to the elbow. Seen from the front, the chest between the forelegs is well filled.
Coat
The Kooikerhondje has a double water-repellent layer of medium length, with smooth, dense layer. The coat may have a slight wave, but is otherwise straight. Featherings are soft on the neck, behind the ears and at the back of the thighs. The ears are feathered, preferably with black bangs. The forelegs are moderately feathered pen but below the hocks.
Color
Different patches of orange-red light over a white background. It is preferable to red as the predominant color. On the head, a white blaze, with red cheeks and ears and ear fringe black is preferred. A white tail is preferred. Disqualifications: Patches of black on a white background, black, white, beige tricolor; albinism.
Temperament
The Kooikerhondje is a cheerful dog, loving equally at home as a hunting dog or a family companion. Kooikers insurance and outgoing with people who know a little concerned but with strange people and dogs. They do well with children who are raised with them. Because of their intelligence and sensitivity, Kooikers require the company but not the dominant style of training.
Gait
The ride is smooth and elastic Kooiker, showing good reach in front and a strong driving rear. Feet should turn neither in nor out and legs travel in a straight line. As speed increases, the dog should single-track, with the topline remaining level.
