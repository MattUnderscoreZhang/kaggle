Overview
Norwegian Lundehund is a small rectangular and agile Spitz breed with unique features not found in any other breed. Originally from the remote islands of the Arctic Norway, the dog was used to fight and live puffin birds recover from the crevices of steep vertical cliffs. To allow the dog to raise, lower, and brake on these cliffs, unique structural characteristics have evolved and must be present to define this race: at least six toes on each foot pads elongated hind legs and an elastic neck that allows the head to bend backwards to touch the spine, letting the dog around in narrow puffin bird caves and shoulders flexible enough to allow the front legs to extend flat to one side in order to embrace the cliffs. This shoulder structure produces a unique rotational movement. Finally, close and fold the ears forward or backward to protect against debris. The temperament is alert, but not expected to be outgoing towards strangers.
Body type
The body is rectangular in shape. The back is strong and straight. The croup slopes slightly. The length of the chest is of medium width, is relatively deep and wide. There is a slight put-up.
Coat
Double layer with a hard outer layer and a dense smooth. The coat is short on the head and front of legs, longer and thicker on the neck and the back of the thighs. Is dense in shallow tail. The male typically has a thicker necklace around her neck. The Lundehund occurs naturally, without clipping.
Color
Black, White, Sable & White, Grey, Red, Yellow
Temperament
A Lundehund is alert, very energetic, loyal and protective. He can be wary of strangers but never aggressive toward people.
Gait
Light and elastic. As the Lundehund is designed to scale cliffs and narrow crevices work, the front assembly must be flexible and broad. This produces an elastic gait one frontal rotating movement. But moves close to the rear side.
