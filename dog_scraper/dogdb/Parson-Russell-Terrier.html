Overview
Parson Russell Terrier is a,, terrier hunting active small to medium sized agile, built to go to ground after fox. The body is slightly longer than tall and capable to be passed through the hands of an average man placed behind the front legs. The length of the back from the withers to set-on of tail is about equal to the height of the dog at the withers. No matter single terrier size, the legs should be long enough to allow the dog to move with speed and agility in difficult terrain. The head is moderately broad, with a flat skull, a stop almost imperceptible, and a powerful cannon that is a little shorter than the skull. The ears are secured to the outer edges of the skull and are V-shaped, falling forward, and lead to the skull. The tail is straight, high, and normally docked, but not nearer the body. The skin is thick and warm, whether rough or smooth, is always dense. The Parson Russell Terrier is mostly white with black spots, beige or black and tan. The Parson Russell Terrier should be evaluated as a working terrier, and exaggerations or faults should be penalized in proportion to how much they interfere with the dog's ability to work. The scars should not be penalized nor regarded as proof of the working capacity of a terrier.
Body type
Adequate provided Parson Russell Terrier is slightly longer than tall. The length of back from withers to set-on of tail is equal to the height, measured from the withers to the ground. The Parson Russell Terrier is perfectly designed to go to ground. This requires a cabinet with a depth sufficient to give a good heart and lung room, but without much depth and width as the dog is encumbered underground. Well sprung ribs extend well back, but should be able to be penetrated from behind the shoulder of the hand of an average man. The chest should be able to be compressed so that the dog is unhindered when working underground. The back is of moderate length, and level, integrated into a muscular, slightly arched loin with slight to moderate tuck-up. The skin is thick.
Coat
Smooth and Broken: Whether smooth or broken, a double coat of good sheen, naturally harsh, close and dense, straight with no suggestion of kink. There is a clear outline with only a hint of eyebrows and beard if natural to the coat. No sculptured furnishings. The terrier is shown in his natural appearance not excessively groomed. Sculpturing is to be severely penalized. Faults: Soft, silky, woolly, or curly topcoat. Lacking undercoat. Excessive grooming and sculpturing.
Color
White, white with black markings, or brown, or a combination of these, tri-color. The colors are clear. While the terrier is predominantly white, moderate body markings should not be criticized. Grizzle is acceptable and should not be confused with brindle. Disqualification: Brands Brindle.
Temperament
Bold and friendly. Athletic and intelligent. At work is a game hunter, tenacious, brave and single-minded. At home, it is playful, exuberant and overwhelmingly affectionate. He is an independent and energetic terrier and requires his attention by. He should not be contentious. Shyness should not be confused with submission. Submission is not a fault. Sparring is not acceptable. Error: shyness. Disqualification: open aggression towards another dog.
Gait
Movement or action is the crucial test of conformation. A tireless ground covering trot showing good reach in front with the hindquarters providing plenty of drive. Pastern break moving forward smoothly with no hint of hackney-like action or goose step. The action is straight in front and behind.
