Overview
A hunting dog of striking color that traditionally brings big game to bay or tree, the Plott is intelligent, alert and confident. Noted for stamina, endurance, agility, determination and aggressiveness in hunting, the powerful, well muscled, but streamlined Plott combines courage with athletic ability.
Body type
The chest is deep and moderately wide elbow with adequate lung space. The ribs are well sprung, tapering to a smart meter in place in the side. Back is broad, well muscled and level. Never roached. Loin muscular and slightly arched. Overall proportion is square or slightly longer than tall, measured from the point of shoulder to point of buttocks and from withers to ground.
Coat
Soft, fine, bright, but thick enough to provide protection against wind and water. rare specimens are double coated, with a short soft coat inside, hidden by a thick outer layer and softer and stiffer.
Color
Red Brindle, Blue Brindle, Brown Brindle, Black, Black Brindle, Tan Brindle
Temperament
Eager to please, loyal, intelligent, alert. Hunter aggressive, bold and courageous. General arrangement, including but varies among strains, with a distinction sometimes appears among the servants for the game and those bred as coonhounds.
Gait
Right and elegant, rhythmic tread. With a large reach in front and behind the unit, the Plott easily traverses various terrains with agility and speed. Legs converge on a single track at speed.
