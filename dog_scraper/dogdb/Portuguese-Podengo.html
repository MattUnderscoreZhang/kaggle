Overview
Portuguese Podengo comes in three sizes: Large, Medium and Small (large, medium and small), and two coat varieties, smooth coated and coated wire. It is a race that is free of exaggeration in all aspects, with a wedge-shaped head, prick ears and a tail like a sickle. The Large and Medium are almost square in proportion and Petit is somewhat rectangular, with a body length of prosternum to point of buttocks approximately 20 percent greater than the height at the withers. In all sizes, depth of chest is half the height at the withers.
Body type
The chest is deep to the elbow, and average width. The sternum is tilted back and up and there sill moderate. The ribs are inclined and slightly arched. The back is straight and long and the back is straight, broad and well muscled. The croup is straight or slightly sloping, broad and muscular. The whole line of the back, from the ears to the tail set, is smooth and fluid. The belly and flanks are lean and there is a slight upward bend.
Coat
There are two varieties of coat:

● Smooth, which is short and very dense, and

● Wire, which is of medium length, rough and tough, not as dense as the coat smooth and coat. The wire coat produces a distinct beard.

The layer shown in the natural state, prepared but not cut or trimmed. The skin is thin and fit.
Color
Yellow and beige a shadow, either solid or mostly white patches or white with patches of any shade of yellow or beige.

In the Small, the colors are also acceptable, but not preferred: shades of black or brown, either solid or with white spots or white with black or brown.
Temperament
