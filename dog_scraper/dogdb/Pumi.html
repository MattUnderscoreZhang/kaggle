Overview
The Pumi is a lively breed dog, medium in size with Terrier characteristics. His Terrier character is most evident in the head, which has an elongated snout and semi-prick ears. This breed is square, with a good amount of leg and long, wavy middle layer forming curls. The Pumi comes in a variety of solid colors.
Body type
The Pumi is exceptionally thin, tense muscles. The chest is deeper than wide, reaching to the elbows and the front of the chest is not pronounced. The ribs are slightly arched. The cross is high, inclined toward a back that is short and straight. The loin is short and tightly bound. The croup is short and slightly sloping. Withdrawn is evident.
Coat
The wavy or curly hair is 1:30 to 3:00 inches long. The coat is long and shaggy shag, never smooth or corded. It is dense and double, with a strong, but not enough, warm and soft under coat. There thick hair, medium length, ears, growing up. The eyes and nostrils are free from long hair. Body hair should be prepared by hand. Scissoring of the face and legs is permitted, but all body hair should not be cut with scissors.

Temperament
Protective, Active, Intelligent, Reserved, Vocal, Lively.

The Pumi is an active dog with a temper bold rather restless. He can be a little suspicious towards strangers. The race is constantly on the alert for action, and can be quite noisy. Shyness is atypical of the breed.
Gait
The gait is light and strong, energetic and efficient, with a moderate scope and hard, allowing them to change direction instantly. The head and tail are carried upward. From the front and rear legs travel in a straight line in the same planes, and tend to converge toward center line of travel as speed increases.
