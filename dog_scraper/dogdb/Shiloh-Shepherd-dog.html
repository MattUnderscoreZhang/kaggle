Overview
The Shiloh Shepherd is a rare breed of dog that is still in development. They are not recognized by any major kennel club, but may be shown in rare breed organizations.

Shilohs are larger and have a straighter back than most modern Alsatians and German Shepherds that are bred for intelligence, temperament and size. Their coats can be a variety of colors and color blends. They compete in obedience, agility and herding events and work as therapy dogs, search and rescue dogs, livestock guardian service and assistance.
Body type
The Shiloh Shepherd is stocky, well-balanced and have a proud carriage and smooth ride, effortlessly. The male Shiloh 30 inches (76 cm) or more in height, with a minimum of 28 inches (71 cm), weighs 120-140 pounds (54-65 kg) with a minimum of 110 pounds (50.5 kg). The female is smaller, standing 28 inches (71 cm) or more in height, with a minimum of 26 inches (66 cm) and weighing 100 to 120 pounds (45 to 54.5 kg) with a minimum of 80 pounds (36 kg). With its proportions even shilohs should appear longer than high.
Coat
Shilohs come in two different coat varieties: smooth or double coat and plush coat. The smooth coat should be of medium length and are found near the body, the hair on the neck and the back of the front and hind legs may be longer and thicker than other areas. The outer coat is dense, straight and harsh. The plush coat is longer, with a soft layer and a "mane" which extends distinctive chest. The body coat should not be more than 5 inches (12.5 cm) long, but are blurred inside the ears and behind the legs should not be more than 3 inches (7.5 cm) of long.

The smooth coat is easy to prepare, although plush coat may shed less. For demonstration purposes, the tufts that grow between the toes and pads should be trimmed. Coats that are open, woolly or curly are serious faults.

Gait
Shiloh's departure is smooth and rhythmic. His long strides and flowing motion require good muscular development. Even while in the flying trot, the straightness of the back should be. The trot is firm and level, without swaying or rocking. To help keep your balance, your feet are brought towards the midline when running. Forward reach of the dog should be as long as possible, ideally extends beyond the nose, giving the impression of Shiloh "fly". Faults in the movement or transport are serious.
