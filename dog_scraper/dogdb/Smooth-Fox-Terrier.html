Overview
The Smooth Fox Terrier is a muscular yet agile, active, small and medium hunting terrier, with enough leg length to run with the hounds on a hunt, and built to go to ground after fox. The body is square and covered with a short, dense coat, smooth. The head is moderately narrow with a flat skull, scarcely perceptible stop, and a powerful muzzle that is equal in length to the skull. Ears are high and are V-shaped, dropping forward, and worn on the head. The tail is straight, set on high, and normally docked, but not close to the body. The coat is predominantly white. The Smooth Fox Terrier is modeled on the symmetry and the power of Fox Hound. When standing, the distance between the front and rear legs is long compared to the relative brevity of the dog's back.
Body type
The Smooth Fox Terrier is square body, measured from point of shoulder to point of buttocks and from withers to the ground. The back is short and level, as part of a short, muscular and slightly arched loin. The chest is deep but not wide. The ribs extend well back, with the front ribs moderately arched and well sprung ribs and deep. The skirt extends to the elbow. The forechest is well filled but does not extend beyond the tip of the shoulder. The croup is nearly flat.
Coat
Coat is short, and close-lying but hard, dense, and abundant. Coat should cover the belly and the inside of the thighs.
Color
White is the predominant color.
Temperament
White should predominate, brindle, red or liver spots are objectionable. Otherwise this point is of little or no importance.
Gait
A Smooth Fox Terrier moves briskly of moderate length. Coming and going, the front and rear legs, rotate parallel and perpendicular to the ground, like the pendulum of a clock. When you move on a loose lead at a moderate speed, hocks and elbows are the same distance as the feet. Viewed from the side, the forelegs reaching well forward, but with little height between the foot and the ground. The oscillating rear leg forwardly ground contacts approximately half the length of the body. As the leg beyond the rump, foot rose quickly, creating a distinctive "boot" of the hock. In movement the back remains level.
