Overview
The Texas Heeler is a crossbred herding dog found mainly in Texas. They are a cross between the Australian Cattle Dog, and the Australian Shepherd, but can be a cross between the ACD and the Border Collie. They can be found in the ranches as cow dogs. As with all hybrid or designer dogs, the Texas Heeler can take after either of the parent or it can have a mix traits from both parents. To determine what is in store for hybrid dogs, it is best to look at characteristics of the parent breeds.
Body type
Texas Heelers are medium-sized dogs that generally weigh between 25 - 50 pounds (11–23 kg).  They usually stand between 17-22 inches (43–61 cm) tall,most have bob-tails but can have long tails.
Coat
Texas Heeler coats vary greatly from short haired and smooth to medium length. Mixed breed coats come in many shapes, colors, textures, and thicknesses.  They are weather resistant. This hybrid dog belongs to an average shedder presumably on sex and geography.

Activity / Care
Spending a lot of time in an activity like regular walking or jogging is required. Inadequate exercise for this breed results to torpor.

They are easy-to-groom and do not need much attention. Bristle tip brush can be use regularly. Bathing can be done only when it is essential.
