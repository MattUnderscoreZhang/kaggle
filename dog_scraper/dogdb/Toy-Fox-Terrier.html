Coat
A distinctive feature of the breed, the coat is short, glossy and shiny, fine texture and soft to touch. It is slightly longer in the ruff (back of the neck and shoulder), uniformly covering the body. Underline, inside the front legs and the lower part of the rear legs are covered with at least a thin layer of hair. The skin is firm but flexible.

Temperament
Cute, lively, smart, good companion dogs.

The Toy Fox Terrier is self-possessed, determined. They are energetic, alive and strong for his size. They are not easily intimidated by other pets. Most are comical, funny and playful all their lives, which is generally large compared to many other breeds. They are friendly and loyal to their masters or owners, but protective. As a general rule, are easy to train and adapt to show in conformation and obedience.
Gait
The movement is smooth and fluid, with legs moving straight and in a line parallel to a walk or slow trot, with your back straight and head and tail up. There is a balance and coordination, with good reach in front and good drive in the rear. Movement is used to assess progress and to evaluate the parties participating in the march, therefore, the points assigned to the movement are included when considering all the structural parts of the dog involved.

In gaiting, stifles, hocks and feet should turn neither in nor out, and hindlegs move in line with the front legs.
