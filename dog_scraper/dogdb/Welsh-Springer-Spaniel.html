Overview
The welsh springer spaniel is a superior water dog, very sharp and hard work, under any environmental conditions it is loyal and willing to work. It has a good sense of smell and can be used for any form of hunting. After a good training, can be compared with any gun dog. As a companion dog, it is the company size, larger than the cocker spaniel and strong, but smaller than English springer spaniel. It is a good guard dog, but on children is usually very mild and other animals.
Body type
The well-developed, muscular chest has a prominent forechest. The brisket reaches to the elbows. The ribs are well-sprung. The topline is level. The slightly arched, muscular loin is close coupled. The croup is very slightly rounded.
Coat
The hair is naturally straight flat and soft to the touch, never wiry or wavy. It is dense enough to be waterproof, anti spine, and weatherproof. The back of the front legs, the hind legs above the hocks, chest and lower body are moderately feathered. The ears and tail are lightly feathered. Coat so excessive as to be an obstacle in the field should be avoided. Obvious Barber is to be avoided as well.
Color
Can only be full of red and white. Allows any type plaques and any white parts can have freckles.
Temperament
The Welsh Springer Spaniel is a distinct variety of other spaniels. Its name is derived from his hunting style. His coat is thick enough to protect it from adverse hunting conditions, but not excessive enough to hinder their work.

The breed is active, a loyal and loving temperament, making it an ideal part of the family as well as an excellent hunting companion. They can be reserved with strangers, but never to the point of being too shy or vicious.
