Overview
The Wetterhoun is a breed of dog traditionally used as a hunting dog for hunting small mammals and waterfowl in the province of Friesland in the Netherlands. The name of the dog comes from the West Frisian Wetterhûn meaning "water dog." Plural of Wetterhoun is Wetterhounen in Dutch. The breed may also be called the Otterhoun or Dutch Spaniel, although it is not a Spaniel-type dog.
Body type
The Wetterhoun is a medium sized dog between 55 and 59 centimeters (21.6 to 23 inches) at the withers. They weigh between 25 and 35 kilos, 55 and 77 lb]). Their fur is thick and curly, except for the head, ears and legs, where the hair is soft, water repellent layer is described as a greasy feel. Coat color may be solid black or brown, or black with white or brown with white, with no markings or roan ticking or white. The texture of the layer should not be woolly, as such, no skin resist water. The ears are set low and hanging flat to the head and the tail curls tightly over the back. The breed has an unusual expression, something dark because of the shape of the eyes, [1], which marks it as different from other dog breeds.
